A Look at the Numbers

How have evictions in San Francisco changed since 1997?

San Francisco's housing crisis is a topic often spoken about with the level of caution used to prevent an egg from cracking. While it's good people recognize the precarious nature of homelessness, that same sensitivity frequently prevents the conversation from advancing beyond agreeing there's a problem and a fix is necessary.

The purpose of this site is:

  • Use d3.js's library to visualize eviction data obtained from SFGOV.
  • Show how evictions have changed since 1997.
  • Serve as a launching pad for data backed conversations.

The purpose of this site is NOT:

  • Proffer a solution.
  • Assign blame.
  • Imply guilt.

Most noticeably, from 1997-2017, Owner Move In evictions have decreased by nearly 50%, while Breach of Contract type evictions have roughly doubled absorbing the difference. These two types of evictions accounted for 44.7% of evictions in 2017.

Following the dotcom bust and financial crisis, evictions fell, which at first seems countercyclical. The number of evictions for 2017, the last recorded full year, is down 27% from the previous year and 55% compared to peak evictions in 1998. However, 2017 is up 22% from 2009's record low. At the time of publication, 2018 is on track to have the fewest evictions recorded.

Taking a more detailed look at how evictions are distributed across neighborhoods in San Francisco, The Mission, Tenderloin, Castro, and Sunset consistently have among the most on an absolute basis. A broad trend that can be gleamed from this is that evictions occur more in the bottom third of the housing market.
